<P style="TEXT-ALIGN: center"><FONT color=#df9e1e>Richmont Mines Reports Strong 
Second Quarter Financial Results; On Track to Meet or Exceed Guidance 
Estimates</FONT></P>
<P style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><B><FONT color=#595959>TORONTO, Ontario, Canada, 
August 8, 2016 </FONT></B><FONT color=#595959>- Richmont Mines Inc. (TSX - NYSE 
MKT: RIC) (&#8220;Richmont&#8221; or the &#8220;Corporation&#8221;) announces 
operating and financial results for the three and six months ended June 30, 
2016, driven by solid results from the Island Gold Mine. The Corporation will 
host a conference call and webcast on Monday, August 8, 2016, beginning at 8:30 
a.m. Eastern Time (details below). </FONT><I><FONT color=#595959>(All amounts 
are in Canadian dollars, unless otherwise indicated.)</FONT></I></P>
<P style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><B><FONT color=#595959>Second Quarter 
Highlights</FONT></B></P>
<UL>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Company-wide production was 23,320 ounces 
for the quarter, an 11% decrease over Q2 2015,</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>primarily due to the depletion of the Monique stockpile earlier 
this year. The Island Gold Mine</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>produced 18,617 
ounces of gold in the second quarter, a 24% increase over Q2 2015, driven 
by</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>record underground and mill productivity of 911 
tonnes per day and 878 tonnes per day,</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>respectively, 
as well as a positive reconciliation (mined vs. reserves) of 19%.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Gold sold during the quarter was 24,888 
ounces, a decrease of 10% over Q2 2015, at an average</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>realized price of $1,628 (US$1,263) per ounce.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Revenues for the quarter were $40.6 million
(US$31.5 million), consistent with Q2 2015.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Company-wide cash costs</FONT><SUP><FONT 
color=#595959>1 </FONT></SUP><FONT color=#595959>for the quarter were $903 per 
ounce (US$701 per ounce), a decrease of</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>7% over Q2 
2015 and below current guidance estimates. Cash costs for the Island Gold Mine 
were</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>$766 per ounce (US$595 per ounce), a 20% 
decrease over Q2 2015 and significantly below current</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>guidance estimates.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Company-wide All-in-Sustaining 
Costs</FONT><SUP><FONT color=#595959>1 </FONT></SUP><FONT 
color=#595959>(&#8220;AISC&#8221;) for the quarter were $1,330 per ounce 
(US$1,032</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>per ounce), in-line with Q2 2015 and within
current guidance estimates. AISC for the Island Gold</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>Mine were $1,038 per ounce (US$806 per ounce), a 21% decrease over
Q2 2015 and significantly</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>below current guidance 
estimates.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Earnings were $2.7 million, 8% lower than 
Q2 2015, or $0.04 per share (US$2.1 million, or US$0.03</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>per share).</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Operating cash flow (after changes in non-
cash working capital) of $14.9 million (US$11.5 million),</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>or $0.25 per share (US$0.19 per share), both in-line with Q2 
2015.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Richmont ended the quarter with an 
increased cash balance of $95.5 million (US$73.4 million),</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>which includes net proceeds of $29.1 million (US$22.7 million) 
related to a bought-deal prospectus</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>financing 
completed on June 7, 2016 and $3.0 million of net free cash 
flow</FONT><SUP><FONT color=#595959>1 </FONT></SUP><FONT 
color=#595959>.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Based on the success of the Phase 1 
exploration program at the Island Gold Mine, the Corporation</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>launched an aggressive 18 to 24 month Phase 2 drilling program of 
up to 142,000 metres, with an</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>estimated 39,000 metres
to be completed in the second half of 2016.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Based on the strong operational and cost 
performance in the first six months of the year, Richmont</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>expects to meet, or exceed, the high end of production guidance 
and the low end of cash cost and</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>AISC guidance. The 
Corporation will determine whether a revision to 2016 guidance estimates 
is</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>warranted following the completion of a scheduled 
3-week electrical upgrade at the Island Gold mill</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>and
the commencement of stope mining in the Q Zone of the Beaufor Mine, both 
expected in</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>August. It is anticipated that any update
to guidance estimates would be released by mid-</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>September.</FONT></P></LI></UL>

<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>The Island Gold Mine produced 18,617 ounces
of gold (20,147 ounces sold), an increase of 24%</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>over
the same period in 2015. As previously disclosed, the mine plan for the quarter 
was focused in</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>lower-grade areas of the mine where 
ore development activities primarily occurred in the lower</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>grade extensions of the second mining horizon. Overall, the 
operation reported a higher than</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>planned mined grade 
of 7.51 g/t. The forward looking 2016 mine plan continues to forecast</FONT> 
<FONT color=#595959>development and stope mining at grades of between 7.0 and 
7.5 g/t gold, using the December 2015</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>resource model 
capped at 95 g/t gold.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Underground productivity averaged a record 
911 tonnes per day and mill processing averaged</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>record productivity of 878 tonnes per day. The higher underground 
productivity and grade realized</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>in the quarter were 
primarily the result of a positive reconciliation (mined vs. reserves) of 
19%,</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>comprised of 8% on tonnes and 10% on grade. As 
compared to Q1 2016, there was a negative</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>impact on 
mined grades as a result of mining wider zones as compared to reserves. It is 
expected</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>that over the coming quarters drilling 
patterns will continue to be evaluated to best optimize grade</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>and tonnage profiles.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>The percentage of higher cost development 
ore versus total ore tonnes mined was 48% for the</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>quarter, as compared to a planned 40%. It is expected that during 
the second half of the year the</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>percentage of 
development ore mined will decrease to planned levels of 40% as stope 
mining</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>commences in the second mining 
horizon.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Cash costs for the quarter were $766 per 
ounce (US$595 per ounce), significantly below guidance</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>estimates and a 20% decrease over the Q2 2015.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>AISC per ounce decreased to $1,038 
(US$806), a decrease of 21% over Q2 2015. AISC for the quarter included $5.4 
million of sustaining capital, comprised of $2.0 million of underground 
development costs, $0.8 million in electrical upgrades, $0.8 million of 
delineation drilling, $0.3million for capital lease payments and $1.5 million on
other assets.</FONT></P></LI></UL>

<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>At the end of the quarter, the Beaufor Mine
reported 2.5 years of operations without lost-time injury.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Production for the quarter was 4,703 ounces
(4,741 ounces sold), a 34% decrease over Q2 2015,</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>primarily as a result of lower than expected tonnes and grades 
mined in secondary Zones M-MF</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>and 12.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Development of the higher-grade Q Zone 
advanced during the quarter with stope mining planned</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>for the third quarter, which should contribute to increased grade 
and ounces produced in the</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>second half of the 
year.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>Cash costs for the quarter were $1,486 per 
ounce (US$1,154 per ounce), a 40% increase over Q2</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>2015. Cash costs are expected to decrease as stope mining in the 
higher grade Q Zone begins in</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>the third
quarter.</FONT></P>
<LI>
<P align=justify><FONT color=#595959>AISC per ounce for the second quarter were 
$1,899 (US$1,475), a 51% increase over Q2 2015.</FONT> <FONT 
color=#595959>Sustaining costs for the quarter were $2.0 million, which included
$1.0 million for capitalized</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>underground mine 
development, $0.4 million for expensed exploration costs and $0.6 million of 
other sustaining costs. AISC are expected to decrease as higher grades stope 
mining in the new Q</FONT> <FONT color=#595959>Zone begins in the second half of
the year.</FONT></P></LI></UL>
