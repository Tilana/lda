font-family:Times New Roman">XTO Energy has advised the trustee that it has 
deducted budgeted development costs of $600,000, production expense of 
$1,832,000 and overhead
of $1,012,000 in determining the royalty payment to the Trust for the current 
month. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-
family:Times New Roman"><U>Arbitration Proceedings &#150; XTO Energy </U></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; 
font-family:Times New Roman">Regarding the $37 million settlement reached by XTO
Energy on the Fankhouser v. XTO Energy, Inc. litigation, XTO Energy notified the
trustee
with the September 2012 distribution that it charged the trust $28.5 million of 
the settlement, of which $23.4 million affected the net proceeds from Oklahoma 
and $5.1 million affected the net proceeds from Kansas. Based on recent revenue 
and
expense levels, XTO Energy has advised the trustee that it expects the 
settlement deductions, if deemed allowed by the arbitrators, to cause costs to 
exceed revenues for approximately 12 months on properties underlying the 
Oklahoma net profits
interests and approximately 7 years on properties underlying the Kansas net 
profits interests; however, changes in oil or natural gas prices or expenses 
could cause the time period to increase or decrease, correspondingly. </P>
<p Style='page-break-before:always'>
<HR  SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; 
font-family:Times New Roman">As mentioned in the second quarter Form 10-Q filed 
on August&nbsp;6, 2013, the trustee disagrees
with XTO Energy that all or any portion of the settlement should be deducted 
from Trust revenues, and the dispute is being arbitrated. The three member panel
of arbitrators (the &#147;Tribunal&#148;) has been named and pleadings have been
filed by
both parties. The arbitration hearing is tentatively scheduled to begin 
November&nbsp;12, 2013 in Fort Worth, Texas if not sooner disposed of by the
parties by agreement or by the Tribunal on motion. The trustee requested that 
the Tribunal enjoin
XTO Energy from continuing to deduct the Fankhouser settlement amount while the 
arbitration is pending. A hearing on the injunction was held on October&nbsp;27,
2012. The Tribunal ordered that pending the issuance of a final award or further
order
of the Tribunal, XTO Energy should not treat any costs or expenses associated 
with the Fankhouser settlement as chargeable against the trust&#146;s net profit
interest under the conveyances. The Tribunal denied the trust&#146;s request for
an
interim order directing XTO Energy to pay the trust the amounts offset against 
the trust&#146;s September and October 2012 distributions on the basis of the 
Fankhouser litigation. Based on this decision, deductions associated with the 
Fankhouser
settlement were suspended starting in November 2012. </P> <P STYLE="margin-
top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New 
Roman"><U>Arbitration Proceedings - Goebel </U></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; 
font-family:Times New Roman">On August&nbsp;12, 2013, a demand for arbitration 
styled <I>Sandra G. Goebel vs. XTO Energy, Inc., Timberland Gathering&nbsp;&amp;
Processing
Company, Inc. and Bank of America, N.A</I>. was filed with the American 
Arbitration Association. The claimant, Sandra Goebel, is a unitholder in the 
trust and alleges that XTO Energy breached the conveyances by misappropriating 
funds from the trust
by failing to modify its existing sales contracts with its affiliate Timberland 
Gathering&nbsp;&amp; Processing Company, Inc. 
(&#147;Timberland&#148;).&nbsp;Goebel alleges that these contracts do not 
currently reflect &#145;market rate&#146; terms,
and that XTO had a duty to renegotiate the contracts to obtain more favorable 
terms. The claimant further alleges that Bank of America breached its fiduciary 
duty by acquiescing and facilitating XTO Energy&#146;s alleged self-dealing and 
concealing
information from unitholders that would have revealed XTO Energy&#146;s 
breaches. The claim also alleges aiding and abetting breach of fiduciary duty by
XTO Energy, and disgorgement and unjust enrichment by Timberland. The claimant 
seeks from the
respondents damages of an estimated $59.6 million for alleged royalty 
underpayments, exemplary damages, an accounting by XTO Energy, a declaration, 
costs, reasonable attorneys&#146; fees, and prejudgment and postjudgment 
interest. The trustee
intends to file a response to the arbitration </P>

that Bank of America shall be indemnified by the trust and shall have no 
liability, other than for fraud, gross
negligence or acts or omissions in bad faith as adjudicated by final 
nonappealable judgment of a court of competent jurisdiction. </P> <P 
STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-
family:Times New Roman">As
previously disclosed, the trustee reserved a total of $900,000 from trust 
distributions to help fund potential legal and other expenses relating to the 
XTO Energy arbitration. As of September 2013, the reserve had been fully 
depleted in connection
with such expenses. Any additional expenses relating to this arbitration will be
deducted as administrative expense when incurred, however a future reserve may 
be established to accommodate payment of these expenses as needed. Additionally,
the
trustee anticipates that the trust will incur additional legal and other
expenses in connection with the Goebel arbitration. As a result, the trustee 
intends to reserve an additional $1.6 million from trust distributions, which it
currently
anticipates taking over a period of four months. The current month&#146;s 
distribution reflects a deduction of $400,000 in connection with such reserve.
