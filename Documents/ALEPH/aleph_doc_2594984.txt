<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times 
New Roman"><B>San Juan Basin Royalty Trust </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; 
margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>News Release

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times 
New Roman">Compass Bank, Trustee </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-
bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">300 West Seventh 
Street, Suite B </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times 
New Roman">Fort Worth, Texas 76102 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-
bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>San 
Juan Basin
Royalty Trust to Review Preliminary Unit Holder Solicitation Materials in 
Connection with Demand for Special Meeting </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; 
margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>FORT WORTH, 
Texas, August 19,
2016 &#150; </B>Compass Bank, as Trustee of the San Juan Basin Royalty Trust 
(NYSE:SJT) (the &#147;Trust&#148;), today confirmed that Southwest Bank and 
Robert Lansford, senior business development officer for Southwest Bank 
(together,
&#147;Southwest Bank&#148;) filed with the U.S. Securities and Exchange 
Commission (&#147;SEC&#148;) a preliminary solicitation statement seeking to 
call a Special Meeting of the Trust&#146;s unit holders to remove Compass Bank 
as trustee and elect
Southwest Bank as successor trustee of the Trust. </P> <P STYLE="margin-
top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Unit 
holders are advised to take no action at this time and to not return any consent
card sent by
Southwest Bank. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-
size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Compass Bank, in consultation with its 
financial and legal advisors, is reviewing Southwest Bank&#146;s preliminary 
solicitation
materials. Compass Bank issued the following statement: </P> <P STYLE="margin-
top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New 
Roman">&#147;We caution unit holders against Southwest Bank&#146;s evident self-
interest in this effort
to elect itself trustee. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; 
font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Compass Bank is an Alabama state-
charted bank with operations throughout the Sunbelt region of the United States.
As trustee of
the Trust for over a decade, Compass Bank remains committed to serving the unit 
holders of the Trust and maximizing unit holder value. Compass Bank&#146;s team 
of trust officers and professionals, some of whom have worked with the Trust for
nearly
25 years, are intimately familiar with all aspects of administering the Trust. 
Compass Bank&#146;s comprehensive, fully-integrated management model includes 
in-depth trust and fiduciary services designed to address any unique business 
circumstance.
The primary trust officer assigned to the Trust is a Texas-licensed attorney who
has served as an oil and gas manager at Compass Bank for several years. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times 
New Roman">In light of low commodity prices and the pending litigation with the 
operator of the subject interests of the Trust, Compass Bank believes that 
continuity of
administration is important, which is why we oppose a change in trusteeship. We 
believe it is in the best interests of the unit holders for Compass Bank to 
continue its longstanding service as trustee of the Trust. Compass Bank will 
continue to
vigorously pursue claims of alleged underpayments and overcharges made by the 
operator of the subject interests. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-
bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Finally, despite 
suggestions made by
Southwest Bank in its solicitation statement, both Compass Bank and its parent 
BBVA believe that there are no existing or potential conflicts of interest in 
serving as the trustee of the Trust, and neither has any intention of divesting 
Compass
Bank&#146;s operation of the Trust.&#148; </P>
