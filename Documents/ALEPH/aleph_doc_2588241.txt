<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" 
style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Announces Completion of Acquisition 
of Remaining White Oak Equity Interests and Updates Guidance</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" 
style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman"
style="font-size:10.0pt;">TULSA, OKLAHOMA, July&nbsp;31, 2015 &#151; Alliance 
Resource Partners, L.P. (NASDAQ: ARLP) today announced that it has acquired from
White Oak Finance,&nbsp;Inc. and other parties all of the equity interests in 
White Oak Resources LLC (&#147;White Oak&#148;) not currently owned by 
ARLP.&#160; Upon closing of the transaction, Alliance WOR Processing, LLC, a 
wholly-owned subsidiary of ARLP, assumed operating control of the White Oak Mine
No.&nbsp;1 (&#147;Mine No.&nbsp;1&#148;), an underground longwall mining 
operation located in Hamilton County,&nbsp;Illinois, which is currently 
producing high-sulfur coal from the Herrin No.&nbsp;6 seam at an annual rate of 
approximately 6 million tons.&#160; As a result of the transaction, ARLP now 
owns 100 percent of the equity interests in White Oak, coal reserves at Mine 
No.&nbsp;1 that are leased to White Oak and the preparation plant and loading 
facilities at Mine No.&nbsp;1.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" 
style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times 
New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Under the terms of the agreement, ARLP paid
$50 million cash at closing, which was funded with </font>cash on hand and 
availability under its current credit facilities.&#160; Additional contingent 
consideration may be due in the future, which ARLP believes has a nominal 
present value based upon current market conditions.</p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" 
style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" 
style="font-size:10.0pt;">Reflecting the acquisition of White Oak and 
ARLP&#146;s results to date and continuing adjustments in response to market 
conditions, the following full-year guidance for 2015 is provided:</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" 
style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt .25in;text-
indent:.5in;"><i><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-
size:10.0pt;font-style:italic;">Coal Production and Sales Volumes &#151; 
</font></i>For the full year 2015, coal production is expected in a range of 
42.8 to 43.5 million tons and sales volumes are expected in a range of 42.7 to 
43.8 million tons, including anticipated coal production and sales volumes from 
Mine No.&nbsp;1 for the remainder of 2015.&#160; ARLP has secured price 
commitments for approximately 42.2 million tons in 2015 and has also secured 
coal sales and price commitments for approximately 31.2 million tons, 13.0 
million tons and 9.6 million tons in 2016, 2017 and 2018, respectively.</p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" 
style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt .25in;text-
indent:.5in;"><i><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-
size:10.0pt;font-style:italic;">Revenue, EBITDA and Net Income Estimates &#151; 
</font></i>ARLP is currently expecting 2015 revenues in a range of $2.37 to 
$2.41 billion, excluding transportation revenues.&#160; Total Revenue estimates 
include additional coal sales revenues from Mine No.&nbsp;1, partially offset by
reduced other sales and operating revenue from the terminated coal royalties and
surface facilities services related to White Oak.</p>

performance from its existing operations will be in line with prior guidance and
that gaining operating and marketing control of Mine No.&nbsp;1 should be 
modestly accretive to EBITDA and net income for the remainder of 2015, however, 
the</font></p>

integration of the White Oak Mine No.&nbsp;1, benefits resulting from the recent
acquisition of mining equipment and optimization efforts across all 
operations.</p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times

the allocation of noncontrolling interest) before net interest expense, income 
taxes and depreciation, depletion and amortization.&#160; EBITDA is used as a 
supplemental financial measure by our management and by external users of our 
financial statements such as investors, commercial banks, research analysts and

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman"

<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-

#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font>the financial performance of our 
assets without regard to financing methods, capital structure or historical cost
basis;</p>
<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-

#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font>our operating performance and return 
on investment as compared to those of other companies in the coal energy sector,
without regard to financing or capital structures; and</p>

is defined as EBITDA excluding equity in income or loss of affiliates, interest 
expense (before capitalized interest), interest income, income taxes and 
estimated maintenance capital expenditures.&#160; Distribution coverage ratio 
(&#147;DCR&#148;) is defined as DCF divided by distributions paid to 
partners.&#160; DCF and DCR are used as supplemental financial measures by our 
management and by external users of our financial statements, such as investors,
commercial banks, research analysts and others, to assess:</font></p>

partner) to fund the cash distributions we expect to pay to unitholders;</p>

optimization efforts across all of our operations.&#160; Our actual maintenance

includes operating expenses, outside coal purchases and other income divided by 
tons sold.&#160; Transportation expenses are excluded as these expenses are 
passed through to our customers and, consequently, we do not realize any margin 
on transportation revenues.&#160; Segment Adjusted EBITDA Expense is used as a 
supplemental financial measure by our management to assess the operating 
performance of our segments.&#160; Segment Adjusted EBITDA Expense is a key 
component of EBITDA in addition to coal sales and other sales and operating 
revenues.&#160; The exclusion of corporate general and administrative expenses 
from Segment Adjusted EBITDA Expense allows management to focus solely on the 
evaluation of segment operating performance as it primarily relates to our 
operating expenses.&#160; Outside coal purchases are included in Segment 
Adjusted EBITDA Expense because tons sold and coal sales include sales from

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times 
New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Segment Adjusted EBITDA per ton is defined
as net income (prior to the allocation of noncontrolling interest) before net 
interest expense, income taxes, depreciation, depletion and amortization and 
general and administrative expenses divided by tons sold.&#160; Segment Adjusted
EBITDA removes the impact of general and administrative expenses from EBITDA 
(discussed above) to allow management to focus solely on the evaluation of 
segment operating performance.</font></p>
