<P style="margin:0px; padding-left:56.667px; padding-right:38.4px; font-
size:14pt" align=center><B>Focus Upgrades Bayovar 12 Phosphate PEA to Pre-
Feasibility Study; Appoints Principal Mining Engineer</B></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=justify>July 27, 2015; Focus 
Ventures Ltd. is pleased to provide an update on its Bayovar12 phosphate rock 
project in Peru, including the addition of an experienced mining engineer to the
team and a change to the scope and timing of on-going engineering studies.</P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt"><I>Building the Focus Team</I></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=justify>Management is pleased to 
announce that Mr. Kevin Ross recently joined the Focus team as its Principal 
Mining Engineer. &nbsp;Mr. Ross&#146;s responsibilities will include overseeing 
the engineering work and, eventually, construction of the project as defined by 
the pre-feasibility study.</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=justify>Mr. Ross is a mining 
engineer with 39 years of operational experience in the mining industry. In a 
broad ranging career, he has worked in North and South America, Europe and 
Africa in the base and precious metals and industrial minerals sectors. &nbsp;He
joined Red Back Mining as COO in December 2007 until its $7B acquisition by 
Kinross Gold Corporation in September 2010. &nbsp;Mr. Ross was previously COO of
New Gold and Ivernia and Managing Director of ARCON International Resources. 
&nbsp;Until May 2015 he was Chief Operating Officer for RB Energy, formed by the
merger of Canada Lithium and Sirocco Mining, where he had been COO since 2011. 
&nbsp;Mr. Ross is a graduate of the Royal School of Mines (London) and an 
accredited European Engineer.</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=justify><I>Preliminary Economic 
Analysis upgraded to Pre-Feasibility Study</I></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=justify>In September 2014, Focus 
announced that it had begun a preliminary economic assessment (PEA) study for 
the Bayovar 12 project examining options for production of 300KT-1Mt per year of
29-30% P<SUB>2</SUB>O<SUB>5</SUB> concentrate by washing and flotation for use 
in phosphoric acid manufacture. &nbsp;Focus is upgrading the scope of the PEA to
a Pre-Feasibility Study (PFS) of production of phosphate rock concentrate for 
use as direct application fertilizer (DAPR) for the reasons outlined below: </P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:-2px; text-indent:24px; width:48px; 
font-family:Symbol; font-size:11pt; float:left">&#183;</P>
<P style="margin:0px; padding-left:48px; text-indent:-2px; font-size:11pt" 
align=justify>Positive mineral beneficiation testwork examining a simple 
flowsheet showed that a DAPR product with a P<SUB>2</SUB>O<SUB>5</SUB> grade of 
<I>circa</I> 24% could be produced from the majority of beds by drum scrubbing, 
desliming and sizing.</P>
<P style="margin:0px; clear:left" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:-2px; text-indent:24px; width:48px; 
font-family:Symbol; font-size:11pt; float:left">&#183;</P>
<P style="margin:0px; padding-left:48px; text-indent:-2px; font-size:11pt" 
align=justify>Production of DAPR would potentially give Focus a shorter 
timeframe to commercial production at a lower CAPEX than production utilizing 
flotation of a P<SUB>2</SUB>O<SUB>5</SUB> concentrate for phosphoric acid 
manufacture.</P>
<P style="margin:0px; clear:left" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:-2px; text-indent:24px; width:48px; 
font-family:Symbol; font-size:11pt; float:left">&#183;</P>
<P style="margin:0px; padding-left:48px; text-indent:-2px; font-size:11pt">The 
Company&#146;s market research on potential markets for DAPR indicates that 
substantial demand exists in a number of South American countries.</P>
<P style="margin:0px; clear:left"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=justify>The Company&#146;s mineral 
processing testwork is continuing. A review of recent results indicates that a 
product grade of <I>circa </I>24% P<SUB>2</SUB>O<SUB>5</SUB> can be achieved via
a relatively simple scrubbing and sizing process. &nbsp;The removal of the 
requirement for a flotation circuit would also lead to a significantly reduced 
level of CAPEX for the project. &nbsp;Management also believes the revised 
project scale will be able to initially use the existing infrastructure around 
the project resulting in further CAPEX reductions, and would be easily scalable 
to a larger production scenario in the future. </P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=justify>Focus&#146;s market research
on potential markets for DAPR has shown that a substantial demand likely exists 
in South America and elsewhere for direct application, reactive phosphate rock. 
DAPR sells at a premium price to acidulation concentrate. &nbsp;Discussions with
potential buyers of the DAPR product have been encouraging enough to support the
change of scope from a PEA to a PFS, modified to focus solely on the initial 
production of 300KT per year of DAPR. </P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=justify>The PFS will build on, and 
modify, the work completed to date for the PEA. Focus&#146;s target date for 
completion of the PFS is the end of 2015. </P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=justify><I>Second Resource 
Estimate</I></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=justify>Golders Associates are 
currently completing the new resource estimation (see Company news release 11 
May, 2015). &nbsp;Focus is expecting to be able to announce the revised 
independent NI 43-101 compliant mineral resource estimate around the end of 
August. </P>

<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; padding-right:0.267px; font-size:11pt" 
align=justify><I>About Focus </I></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; padding-right:0.267px; font-size:11pt" align=justify>Focus
is an exploration and development company with two main assets, both located in 
Peru: &nbsp;the Bayovar 12 phosphate deposit located in the Sechura district of 
northern Peru, and the Aurora porphyry copper-molybdenum property located in the
Department of Cusco, which it is developing in a joint venture with Daewoo 
International Corporation.</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; padding-right:0.267px; font-size:11pt" 
align=justify>Phosphate rock is a raw material for fertilizers and vital to 
world food production. &nbsp;Focus is acquiring and developing quality phosphate
projects in Latin America where the discovery and development of new deposits is
becoming increasingly important given the growing demand and limited local 
supply of phosphate for fertilizer production. &nbsp;For further information, 
please call 604-630-5544 or visit our web site <FONT 
style="color:#0000FF"><U>www.focusventuresltd.com</U></FONT>. </P>
