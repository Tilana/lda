<H5 align="left" style="page-break-before:always">&nbsp;</H5><P>
<DIV style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',Times,serif">
<DIV align="right" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 
12pt"><B>Exhibit&nbsp;99.1</B>
</DIV>
<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 12pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>STONE ENERGY 
CORPORATION</B>
</DIV>
<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 14pt; margin-top: 6pt"><B>Announces 
Extension of Bank Waivers and Operational Update</B><BR>
</DIV>
<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 12pt">LAFAYETTE, LA. December&nbsp;19, 
2005</DIV>
<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 
12pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stone Energy Corporation (NYSE: SGY) 
announced today that it has received an extension of
waivers from its bank group. Stone previously announced that the filing of its 
2005 third quarter
10-Q and the restated annual financial statements for 2004 and prior periods 
would be delayed until
March&nbsp;2006, enabling Stone to have its reserves as of December&nbsp;31, 
2005 audited by nationally
recognized reserve consulting firms, including a majority of the reserves being
fully engineered by
these third party firms. Consistent with the revised schedule, the original 
waivers from the
lenders under its bank credit facility through December&nbsp;15, 2005 were 
extended until March&nbsp;31,
2006. Additionally, Stone has agreed to secure borrowings under its bank credit 
agreement with a
security interest in its oil and gas properties. Stone currently has a borrowing
base under the
credit agreement of $300&nbsp;million, $176&nbsp;million of which is borrowed or
committed, leaving $124
million of availability.
</DIV>
<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 
6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Regarding production volumes, Stone is 
currently producing just under 200&nbsp;million cubic feet
of natural gas equivalent (MMcfe) per day and now expects to exit the year 
between 200-210 MMcfe
per day. The fourth quarter and full year 2005 production volumes should average
approximately 158
MMcfe per day and 228 MMcfe per day, respectively. These production figures are 
less than the
previous estimates as a number of non-operated and third party mid-stream 
projects were delayed.
During the first quarter of 2006, Stone expects to reach between 225-250 MMcfe 
per day as more
fields impacted by the hurricanes are brought back on line.
</DIV>
<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 
6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stone currently has a number of exploration 
wells being drilled including its deepwater West
Covington prospect on Green Canyon Block 766 (50% working interest (WI)), three 
deep shelf
prospects &#151; the Doubloon Prospect on Grand Isle Block 106 (50% WI), the 
Elizabeth Prospect on
South Marsh Island Block 230 (50% WI), and the Omega Prospect on Ship Shoal 
Block 199 (75% WI), a
deep onshore Louisiana prospect in the Riceville field (100% WI), and a 
Williston Basin exploration
play. Stone also expects to spud the Patricia Prospect on South Marsh Island 
Block 231 (70% WI) in
early January, which is also a deep shelf exploratory test. Results from these 
wells are expected
late in the first quarter or early second quarter of 2006.
</DIV>
<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 
6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stone Energy is an independent oil and gas 
company headquartered in Lafayette, Louisiana, and
is engaged in the acquisition and subsequent exploration, development, operation
and production of
oil and gas properties located in the conventional shelf of the Gulf of Mexico 
(GOM), deep shelf of
the GOM, deep water of the GOM, Rocky Mountain basins and the Williston Basin. 
For additional
information, please contact Kenneth H. Beer, Senior Vice President and Chief 
Financial Officer, at
337-521-2210-phone, 337-237-0426-fax or via e-mail at CFO@StoneEnergy.com.
</DIV>
