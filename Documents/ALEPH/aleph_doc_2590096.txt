<p style="margin:0px; font-family:Arial; font-size:14pt" 
align="justify"><b>PetroRiver De-Risks Larne Basin with Discovery of Thermogenic
Gas in Woodburn Forest #1-Well</b></p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify"><b><i>Partners To Commence Phase II of Exploration 
Program</i></b></p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify">NEW YORK, NY <font style="font-family:Arial Unicode 
MS,Arial">&ndash;</font> August 4, 2016 <font style="font-family:Arial Unicode 
MS,Arial">&ndash;</font> Petro River Oil Corp. (OTCBB: PTRC) (<font style="font-
family:Arial Unicode MS,Arial">&ldquo;</font>Petro River<font style="font-
family:Arial Unicode MS,Arial">&rdquo;</font> or the <font style="font-
family:Arial Unicode MS,Arial">&ldquo;</font>Company<font style="font-
family:Arial Unicode MS,Arial">&rdquo;</font>) today announced that the analysis
of its first exploratory well <font style="font-family:Arial Unicode 
MS,Arial">&ndash;</font> Woodburn Forest #1 <font style="font-family:Arial 
Unicode MS,Arial">&ndash;</font> located in Larne Basin, Northern Ireland, 
confirmed the presence of an active petroleum system in the Larne Basin. The
drilling of the well detected background gas in the drilling fluid, and samples 
were sent to Applied Petroleum Technology<u> UK Ltd</u> (<font style="font-
family:Arial Unicode MS,Arial">&ldquo;</font>APT<font style="font-family:Arial 
Unicode MS,Arial">&rdquo;</font>), an internationally-recognized consultancy 
with an independent geochemical laboratory in Oslo, Norway.</p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify">APT<font style="font-family:Arial Unicode 
MS,Arial">&rsquo;</font>s report<b style="color:#FF0000;font-weight:normal;"> 
</b>determined that the gas was thermogenic, likely originating from deeper 
Carboniferous shales. The samples were similar to a gas sample from a producing 
gas field in Lancashire, England, just across the North Channel.</p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify">The discovery of thermogenic gas limits the risks associated 
with Petro River<font style="font-family:Arial Unicode MS,Arial">&rsquo;</font>s
Larne Basin exploration program by providing further evidence of a mature source
rock within the basin, and supports Petro River<font style="font-family:Arial 
Unicode MS,Arial">&rsquo;</font>s prior estimates of prospective resources of 
more than 1 billion barrels of potential oil and gas equivalents across the 
license. Carboniferous-sourced basins throughout Europe have historically been 
very prolific. By fulfilling the current work program, the onshore license has 
been extended for a five-year period, which affords ample time for the Company 
to work with Horizon Energy Partners, LLC (<font style="font-family:Arial 
Unicode MS,Arial">&ldquo;</font>Horizon Energy<font style="font-family:Arial 
Unicode MS,Arial">&rdquo;</font>) and other partners to develop Phase II of the 
exploration program, which includes the following: &nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify"><b><i>Reprocess 2-D seismic data</i></b><i>.</i> The joint 
venture partners are currently reprocessing a closely-spaced 2-D grid of 1,100 
line kilometers of offshore seismic data acquired in 2009. &nbsp;When finalized,
the reprocessing should provide clearer structural imaging within the 
Carboniferous, better trap definition in the Triassic and Permian and possibly 
show hydrocarbon indicators in the shallower formations. We intend to <font 
style="font-family:Arial Unicode MS,Arial">&ldquo;</font>shoot<font style="font-
family:Arial Unicode MS,Arial">&rdquo;</font> several 2D tie lines which will 
tie the offshore and onshore data sets. Management estimates completion of 2-D 
seismic reprocessing by the end of October, 2016.</p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify"><b><i>Reprocess 3-D seismic</i></b><i>.</i> A small 3-D survey 
in the area, originally acquired from a nearby salt storage project, may also be
reprocessed during the next several months. </p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify"><b><i>Evaluation of Offshore Drilling Program</i></b><i>.</i> 
The offshore portion of the basin does not contain surface basalts. As such, the
seismic data quality is markedly better. &nbsp;The next steps in the exploration
effort may be acquisition of a new 3-D seismic or further exploratory drilling 
<font style="font-family:Arial Unicode MS,Arial">&ndash;</font> funded either 
internally or through a farm-out transaction. </p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify"><b><u>Background:</u></b></p>
<p style="margin:8px" align="justify"><br></p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="center">-1 of 3-</p>
<p style="margin:8px" align="justify"><br></p>
<hr style="margin-bottom: 9.6px; padding-top: 9.6px;" noshade size="1"><p 
style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial,Times New Roman; 
font-size:12pt; page-break-before:always" align="justify">Petro River owns a 9% 
direct working interest and a 3.2% indirect interest through its investment in 
Horizon Energy in both an onshore license (PL1/10) and offshore license (P2123),
which together comprise about 130,000 acres and encompass the heart of the Larne
Basin. Good reservoir quality had been confirmed from prior drilling and the 
existence of hydrocarbon traps was evident from recently acquired 2-D seismic 
data. </p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify">In May, 2016, the Woodburn Forest #1 well commenced drilling.
&nbsp;The primary objective of the well for Petro River was to confirm the 
existence and maturity of the Carboniferous source rock for the conventional 
reservoirs, and the secondary objective was to test three potential hydrocarbon-
bearing objective zones. The well reached total depth in mid-June 2016. The 
Carboniferous-aged shales were expected to be encountered at approximately 1,550
meters. Unexpectedly, the overlying Permian section thickened considerably and 
at 2,000 meters the well was still drilling in the basal Permian. &nbsp;After 
stopping at 2,000 meters and conducting basic evaluation tests (electric logs, a
check-shot survey, imaging tool, etc.) and assessing the information gained from
these tests, the consensus decision was made not to drill deeper. As a result, 
the Carboniferous section was not directly evaluated. </p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify">During the drilling of the Woodburn Forest #1 well through the 
Permian section background gas shows were present in the drilling fluids. 
Samples were collected and analyzed by APT, who determined that the gas was 
thermogenic and originated from the Carboniferous. &nbsp;The gas sample 
contained carbon isotopes from C-1 to C-6 and the maturity indicated it to be at
the top of the gas window, implying a burial at the time of generation of 
approximately 4,000 meters and a temperature of approximately 150 degrees 
Centigrade. These results evidenced a mature source rock from which hydrocarbons
have migrated, and therefore supports the existence of an active petroleum 
system in the Larne Basin. </p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify">Based on the new Woodburn Forest #1 well data, which furthers 
our understanding of the petroleum system, reservoir deposition, and trapping 
mechanisms, the Company believes that substantial progress has been made in 
moving the Larne Basin project toward eventual commerciality. This was the first
exploration drilled in the Larne Basin in 45 years.</p>
<p style="margin-top:8px; margin-bottom:8px; font-family:Arial; font-size:12pt" 
align="justify">Historically, large basinal discoveries are found and developed 
only after thorough analysis of technical data derived from both seismic 
acquisition and drilling of wells. The Larne Basin continues to be a very 
attractive exploration opportunity based on technical merit, modest exploration 
costs, low political risk and excellent fiscal terms.</p>
