<P style="margin:0px"><U>Description of Business</U></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>Endurance Exploration Group, Inc. (formerly 
Tecton Corporation) (the &#147;Company&#148;) was incorporated under the laws of
the State of Nevada on January 19, 2006, as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Hemis 
Corporation. &nbsp;On December 1, 2006 Hemis declared a dividend of Tecton 
shares to all shareholders as of that date and concurrently cancelled its share 
ownership in the Company. &nbsp;The effect of this dividend declaration and 
share cancellation was that Tecton was spun off as an independent company.</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>&nbsp;&nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><FONT style="background-color:#FFFFFF">On 
November 8, 2013, the Board of Directors approved a change of the Company&#146;s
fiscal year from January 31, to December 31. &nbsp;This Form 10Q for the three 
and nine months ended September 30, 2014 represents the third quarterly filing 
for the new fiscal year. &nbsp;Since both entities were under common control, 
the comparative financial statements are presented as if the previously 
separated entities were combined.</FONT></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>On December 31, 2013, the Company acquired 
100% of the membership interests of Endurance Exploration Group, LLC 
(&#147;Endurance LLC&#148;), a Florida limited liability company, in exchange
for 20,550,539 shares of the Company&#146;s Common Stock being issued to the 
former members. &nbsp;Endurance LLC is now a wholly owned subsidiary with its 
operations being the Company&#146;s primary focus. &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>Endurance LLC is engaged in the 
archaeologically sensitive exploration and recovery of deep-ocean shipwrecks 
throughout the world. &nbsp;The Company intends to recover bullion, precious 
metals, numismatic-grade coinage, high-value non-ferrous metals and other 
valuable cargos from both historic and modern shipwrecks.</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>On January 2, 2014, the Company changed its 
name to Endurance Exploration Group, Inc.</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>Our corporate headquarters are located in 
Clearwater, Florida.</P>

<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>

maturity date of three months or less to be cash equivalents. </P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><U>Fixed Assets</U><B> </B></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>Fixed assets are stated at historical cost. 
Depreciation is provided using the straight-line method at rates based on the 
assets&#146; estimated useful lives which are normally between three and ten 
years. &nbsp;When retired or otherwise disposed, the related carrying value and 
accumulated depreciation are removed from the respective accounts and the net 
difference less any amount realized from disposition, is reflected in earnings. 
Leasehold improvements are amortized over their estimated useful lives or lease 
term, if shorter. Equipment and major overhaul items (such as engines or 
generators) that enhance or extend the useful life of vessel related assets 
qualify to be capitalized and depreciated over the useful life or remaining life
of that asset, whichever is shorter. Certain major repair items required by 
industry standards to ensure a vessel&#146;s seaworthiness also qualify to be 
capitalized and depreciated over the period of time until the next scheduled 
planned major maintenance for that item. All other repairs and maintenance are 
accounted for under the direct-expensing method and are expensed when incurred. 
</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><U>Income Taxes</U></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><FONT style="background-color:#FFFFFF">The 
Company uses the liability method to record income tax activity. Deferred taxes 
are determined based upon the estimated future tax effects of differences 
between the financial reporting and tax reporting bases of assets and 
liabilities given the provisions of currently enacted tax laws.</FONT></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>The accounting for uncertainty in income 
taxes recognized in an enterprise&#146;s financial statements uses the threshold
of more-likely-than-not to be sustained upon examination for inclusion or 
exclusion. Measurement of the tax uncertainty occurs if the recognition 
threshold has been met. </P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><U>Net Earnings (Losses) Per Common Share</U></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>The Company computes earnings (loss) per 
share by dividing net earnings (loss) by the weighted average number of shares 
of common stock and dilutive common stock equivalents outstanding during the 
year. Dilutive common stock equivalents may&nbsp;consist of shares issuable upon
conversion of convertible preferred shares and the exercise of the Company's 
stock options (calculated using the treasury stock method). &nbsp;As of 
September 30, 2014, there were 5,000,000 common stock equivalents that were 
anti-dilutive and were not included in the calculation. Common stock issuable is
considered outstanding as of the original approval date for purposes of earnings
per share computations.</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><U>Fair Value of Financial Instruments</U></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>The fair value of financial instruments, 
which include cash, loans receivable, accounts payable and accrued expenses and 
advances from related parties were estimated to approximate their carrying 
values due to the immediate or short-term maturity of these financial
instruments. Management is of the opinion that the Company is not exposed to 
significant interest, currency or credit risks arising from financial 
instruments.</P>

<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>

been prepared on a going concern basis, which contemplates the realization of 
assets and the satisfaction of liabilities in the normal course of business. As 
shown in the accompanying financial statements for the period January 19, 2006 
(date of inception) to September 30, 2014, the Company incurred losses of 
$5,443,877. The Company has minimal liquid assets. These factors, among others, 
indicate that the Company will be unable to continue as a going concern for a 
reasonable period of time.</P>

management's ability to develop profitable operations and resolve its liquidity

concern.</P>

<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><B>Plan of Operation</B></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>We intend to continue survey operations in 
fall of 2014. Side-scan sonar survey operations on Project 
<I>&#147;Sailfish&#148;</I> began in July of 2013. Over the course of this 
initial survey, approximately 700 square miles were digitally mapped. This sonar
imagery was then post-processed and evaluated for potential targets. In of 2014 
we believe we positively identified the target of Project 
&#147;<I>Sailfish</I>,&#148; the Steamship <I>S.S. Connaught</I>. &nbsp;We 
promptly filed a complaint in U.S. District Court for the Middle District of 
Florida, Tampa Division, (the &#147;Court&#148;) requesting, pursuant to 
admiralty law, that the shipwreck be arrested, that we be appointed substitute 
custodian of the shipwreck and that we be awarded a salvage claim or title to 
the shipwreck and its cargo and, if such rights are granted, we intend to return
to the site in the Spring or Summer of 2015 to conduct a recovery operation 
estimated to take 60 days.</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>As of the date of this filing, the Court has
issued the Warrant for Arrest of the shipwreck. &nbsp;Artifacts from the <I>SS 
Connaught</I>, in the form of iron hull fragments, were delivered to the U.S. 
Marshall as evidence of the arrest. &nbsp;Pursuant to a Motion for Appointment 
of Substitute Custodian, Endurance was named as Substitute Custodian for the 
U.S. Marshalls and re-took possession of the hull fragments mentioned above. 
&nbsp;A status conference with the Court is expected to be scheduled within 45 
days. &nbsp;The Company anticipates that it will be required to provide a 90-day
notice to all potential claimants by way of publication in the United States and
in the United Kingdom. &nbsp;While there can be no guarantee, the Company 
believes it is likely that, as a privately owned vessel that sank in 1860, there
will be no claims with respect to any privately owned and recovered valuables. 
&nbsp;The shipment of gold coins, if recovered, is believed to be British 
government property. &nbsp;Based upon historic precedent the Company also 
believes it will be able to secure, conserve and market the coins with a 
majority of the value being granted to the Company by way of an agreement with 
the British government. &nbsp;As requested in the original filing with the 
Court, Endurance believes it is likely that it will be designated salvor-in-
possession of the wreck or may receive full and clear title to the wreck 
depending upon the filing of claims and the nature of claims filed.</P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=justify>As of November 2013, we finalized a contract
with the sovereign government of an island nation in the Indian Ocean. The 
provisions allow for a three-year period in which to operate within the 
territorial and surrounding waters of the nation with full permissions to survey
for and recover the <I>&#147;Black Marlin&#148;</I> and her silver cargo.
Proceeds from recovery and sale are subject to a graduated distribution, with 
100% first going to cover expedition costs and with a 70% / 30% split thereafter
in our favor. We expect to begin initial survey operations on project 
<I>&#147;Black Marlin&#148;</I> in mid-2014.</P>
