Diamond Corporation's Fiscal 2014 Second Quarter Results
to be released Wednesday, September 4, 2013</B></P>
<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 9.75pt 0">TORONTO, 
Aug. 21, 2013 /CNW/ - Dominion Diamond Corporation
(TSX: DDC, NYSE: DDC) (the &quot;Company&quot;) will release its Fiscal 2014 
Second Quarter results for the period ended July 31,
2013, after market hours on Wednesday, September 4th, 2013.</P>
<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 9.75pt 0">Beginning 
at 8:30AM (ET) on Thursday, September 5th, the Company
will host a conference call for analysts, investors and other interested 
parties. Listeners may access a live broadcast of the
conference call on the Company's web site at www.ddcorp.ca or by dialing 866-
270-6057 within North America or 617-213-8891 from
international locations and entering passcode 66211452.</P>
<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 9.75pt 0">An online 
archive of the broadcast will be available by accessing
the Company's web site at www.ddcorp.ca. A telephone replay of the call will be 
available one hour after the call through 11:00PM
(ET), Thursday, September 19th, 2013 by dialing 888-286-8010 within North 
America or 617-801-6888 from international locations
and entering passcode 15589624.</P>
<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 9.75pt 0"><B><I>About
Dominion Diamond Corporation </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 9.75pt 0"><I>Dominion
Diamond Corporation is a Canadian diamond mining
company with ownership interests in two of the world's most valuable diamond 
mines. Both mines are located in the low political
risk environment of the Northwest Territories of Canada. The Company is the 
fourth largest diamond producer by value globally and
the largest diamond mining company by market capitalization, listed on the 
Toronto and New York Stock Exchanges. </I></P>
<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 9.75pt 0"><I>The 
Company operates the Ekati Diamond Mine through its
80% ownership as well as a 58.8% ownership in the surrounding areas containing 
prospective resources.&nbsp; It also sells diamonds
from its 40% ownership in the Diavik Diamond Mine.</I></P>
