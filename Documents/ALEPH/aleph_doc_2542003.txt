<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><B>Oromin 
Explorations Ltd.</B> (TSX-V:OLE) is pleased to report very encouraging gold 
results from the initial sampling program that forms part of the ongoing first 
phase of its USD$8 million comprehensive exploration program at the 230 square 
kilometre Sabodala Property in eastern S&#233;n&#233;gal.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify>Trench, rock 
and soil geochemical sampling has confirmed historical results and appears to 
correlate well with induced polarisation (&#147;IP&#148;) chargeability 
anomalies identified by Oromin in the initial phase of its geophysical 
evaluation of Sabodala. &nbsp;In addition to showing an excellent correlation 
between strong gold results and the IP anomalies in areas of past and present 
artisanal gold mining activities, Oromin has identified numerous new gold-in-
soil geochemical and IP anomalies.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify>Although it is 
still late in the rainy season at Sabodala, field crews have resumed the Phase I
exploration program and have now begun mechanised trenching, the expansion of 
geochemical sampling coverage and ground IP geophysics, and are completing camp 
construction in preparation for the commencement of drilling.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify>Within a 200 by
250 metre area at Golouma, Oromin excavated three hand trenches near an old 
trench sampled in 2004 by Oromin&#146;s consulting geologist. A series of 
shallow hand trenches within the same area were sampled in 1987 by BRGM 
(Geologic Branch of the French Government). &nbsp;The following summarizes these
results:</P>

<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify>Trench OLE-01, 
which ended within mineralization, is located approximately 250 metres east of 
trench OLE-03. &nbsp;Trench OLE-02, located approximately 100 metres east of 
trench OLE-03, ends in mineralization as the anomalous mineralization occurs at 
the end of the trench exposure. &nbsp;An 8 metre wide section within the centre 
of trench OLE-02 could not be sampled as the trenching crew was unable to reach 
bedrock.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify>All of the 
historical trenches within the 200 by 250 metre area sampled by BRGM did not 
expose the full width of the mineralization. &nbsp;This area has not been 
previously drilled. &nbsp;Additional trenching, including mechanised excavation 
to accelerate the pace of trenching, will be undertaken to extend and deepen 
trenches OLE-01 and OLE-02 and to explore for additional on-strike extensions of
the mineralised intervals.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman">While prospecting, mapping 
and sampling the Golouma grid, Oromin discovered a 100 metre by 200 metre area 
of extensive trenching, artisanal pitting and manual excavations located 
approximately 600 to 800 metres east of trenches OLE-01 to OLE-03. &nbsp;This 
area of artisanal workings, which has not been previously drilled, shows 
excellent correlation with the recently identified IP geophysical anomalies. 
&nbsp;A total of 37 widely-dispersed prospecting samples were collected from the
various rock dumps, outcrops and trenches throughout this area, returning values
ranging from below detection to 27.54 g/t gold. &nbsp;Eighteen of the samples 
returned greater than 1.0 g/t gold, of which thirteen samples were greater than 
3.0 g/t gold and the following eight of which were in excess of 10.0 g/t gold: 
12.83, 17.97, 18.11,21.16, 22.12, 24.14, 24.25 and 27.54 g/t gold. 
&nbsp;Additional excavations, trenches and pits were observed beyond the area 
currently sampled and
 will be followed-up during the current field program.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify>Oromin has also
received results for approximately 75% of the 1,300 soil geochemical samples 
collected prior to the onset of the rainy season. &nbsp;To date, less than 5% 
(approximately 12 square kilometres) of the 230 square kilometre Sabodala 
Property has been explored with ground geophysics, prospecting and soil 
sampling. &nbsp;So far, this exploration coverage includes a 2,400 by 3,000
metre area at Golouma, an 1,800 by 3,000 metre area at South Sabodala and two 
orientation test lines covering both the Sabodala deposit owned by Mineral 
Deposits Limited (&#147;MDL&#148;) and Oromin&#146;s Masato area. &nbsp;The 
results received so far outline a series of extensive gold-in-soil geochemical 
anomalies that correlate well with induced polarisation chargeability anomalies 
identified by Oromin in the initial phase of its geophysical evaluation of the 
Golouma, Masato, South Sabodala and Maka Madina areas. </P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify>At Golouma, 
soil samples as high as 8.0 g/t gold and 12.0 g/t gold occur within a highly 
anomalous area covering a minimum extent of approximately 1.5 by 2 kilometres. 
&nbsp;As noted above, this area has not been previously drilled. &nbsp;This 
strong, broad series of geochemical anomaly covers and extends well beyond the 
area of Oromin&#146;s initial three trenches and the area of artisanal 
excavations. &nbsp;For example, a 500 metre section of grid line 26+00S at the 
site of Oromin&#146;s three hand trenches returned the following sequence of 
highly anomalous soil geochemical results at 50 metre spacing: 0.17, 1.51,0.13, 
2.13, 0.23, 1.37, 3.94, 0.10, 2.43 and 0.28 g/t gold.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify>Immediately 
along trend to the south of MDL&#146;s Sabodala South deposit that lies 300 
metres from Oromin&#146;s concession boundary, a broad soil geochemical anomaly 
is present. This anomaly measures approximately 400 metres in width and extends 
to at least 1,800 metres south of the concession boundary with MDL to the Maka 
Madina area where historical rock sampling by BRGM returned 64 g/t gold. 
&nbsp;Oromin has identified a second, sub-parallel soil anomaly measuring 
approximately 300 metres in width and extending to at least 1,200 metres south 
of the concession boundary with MDL that has returned soil results as high as 
1.6 g/t gold. &nbsp;As at Golouma, there is an excellent correlation between the
gold-in-soil geochemical anomalies and the recently identified IP geophysical 
anomalies, none of which have been previously drilled. Historical drill results 
obtained by BRGM from the Sabodala South deposit include 3.5 g/t gold over 32.0 
metres.<
/P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify>Oromin has also
completed two orientation test lines that crossed MDL&#146;s adjacent Sabodala 
deposit and Oromin&#146;s Masato prospect. &nbsp;Results from these test lines 
have confirmed coincident IP geophysical anomalies with soil geochemical 
anomalies at both the Sabodala deposit and the Masato prospect. &nbsp;&nbsp;This
geophysical and geochemical correlation will assist Oromin in its exploration of
the various targets throughout its Sabodala Property. &nbsp;Soil results from 
the Masato prospect have outlined a 350 metre wide gold-in-soil geochemical 
anomaly that crosses both of the 200-metre spaced orientation test lines. 
&nbsp;This anomaly coincides with an IP geophysical anomaly that correlates well
with a broad area of favourable looking alteration, previous trenching and 
limited historical drilling by BRGM that returned results of up to 12.0 g/t gold
over 5.3 metres and 7.85 g/t gold over 4.1 metres.</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman" align=justify>In addition to 
the resumption of ground geophysics, further prospecting, trenching and 
geochemical sampling at the high-priority areas of the Sabodala Property 
explored thus far, Oromin will begin to evaluate a number of other areas on this
large property.</P>

<P style="margin:0pt; padding-left:72pt; font-family:Garamond" align=justify>The

Sabodala Property in the Republic of S&#233;n&#233;gal. &nbsp;The Sabodala

venture between the Issuer and Bendon International Ltd. (&#147;Bendon&#148;), 
each as to 50% with the Issuer and Bendon each holding a 6.5% interest in trust

agreement on the same terms as the current joint venture agreement between the 
Issuer and Bendon, such joint venture interest to be transferred to Badr on 
execution of such tripartite agreement. &nbsp;The OJVG the sole right to acquire
a 100% interest in the Sabodala Property from the Government of 
S&#233;n&#233;gal by spending at least USD$8 million on exploration by April 17,
2007. &nbsp;Under 
the terms of the OJVG joint venture agreement, Bendon will provide the initial 
USD$2.8 million in exploration expenditures with the Issuer providing the 
subsequent USD$5.2 million commitment. &nbsp;&nbsp;Bendon has agreed to arrange 
financing, through an equity placement in the Issuer, of at least USD$4 million 
of the Issuer&#146;s expenditure commitment. &nbsp;Bendon will also take a lead 
role in arranging any production financing that the OJVG may require. &nbsp;Badr
holds a free carried interest until the initial USD$8 million commitment is 
completed, at which time the three parties will bear all future costs associated
with the exploration and development of the Sabodala Property on a pro rata 
basis, or be subject to dilution.</P>
